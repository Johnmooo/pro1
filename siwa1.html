The Mountain of the Dead

The Mountain of the Dead, located in Siwa Oasis, is an ancient archaeological site known for its rock-cut tombs. These tombs date back to the 26th dynasty, as well as the Greco-Roman period. The Salt Lakes

The Salt Lakes in Siwa Oasis are stunning natural attractions, famous for their crystal-clear waters and high salt concentration, reaching around 98%. These unique lakes are not only visually captivating but also believed to have therapeutic benefits, helping with skin conditions like psoriasis and boosting relaxation. The dense salt levels allow visitors to float effortlessly on the surface, making swimming a one-of-a-kind experience. Additionally , the lakes contribute to local industries by providing salt for various uses.

Cleopatra's Spring

Cleopatra's Spring, located in Siwa Oasis, is a natural hot spring with historical and cultural significance. It is said that Queen Cleopatra herself bathed in this spring during her visit to Siwa. The spring is surrounded by lush greenery and is a popular spot for relaxation and rejuvenation. Its warm, mineral-rich waters are believed to have therapeutic properties, making it a favorite destination for both locals and tourists.

The Green Mountain

The Green Mountain in Siwa Oasis is a natural landmark known for its unique beauty and serene environment. It is surrounded by lush greenery, palm trees, and olive groves, making it a peaceful retreat for visitors. The mountain offers stunning panoramic views of the oasis and is a popular spot for hiking and relaxation. Its natural charm and tranquil atmosphere make it a must-visit destination for those exploring Siwa.

Al-Shali Fortress

Shali Fortress, located in Siwa Oasis, Egypt, is a historic mud-brick structure built in the 13th century. It served as a fortified shelter for the local community, offering protection against invasions. The fortress is constructed from "kershef," a unique blend of salt and mud found in the region. Despite being partially eroded by heavy rains, its ruins stand as a symbol of Siwa's cultural and architectural heritage. Visitors can wander through its ancient pathways and enjoy breathtaking views of the oasis from higher points within the site.
