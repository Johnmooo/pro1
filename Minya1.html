Mallawi Museum

Mallawi Museum is an archaeological museum in Minya, Egypt, opened in 1962. It showcases artifacts from the Pharaonic, Greek, Roman, Coptic, and Islamic eras, including statues, coffins, papyri, and pottery. After being vandalized in 2013, it was fully restored and reopened in 2016.

Tell el-Amarna

Tell el-Amarna is an archaeological site in Minya, Egypt. It was the capital of Egypt during Akhenaten's reign in the 14th century BC, known for the worship of the god "Aten." The site includes temples, palaces, and royal tombs, showcasing the unique art of that era.

Beni Hasan

Beni Hasan is an archaeological site in Minya, featuring rock-cut tombs from the Middle Kingdom (c. 2000–1800 BC). The tombs are famous for their detailed carvings depicting daily life, sports, warfare, and hunting, offering valuable insights into ancient Egyptian culture.

Deir Abu Fana

Deir Abu Fana is an ancient monastery located west of Mallawi in Minya, dating back to the 4th century AD. It is one of Egypt’s oldest monasteries, featuring an ancient church adorned with Coptic frescoes. The monastery is known for its monastic heritage and pilgrimage visits and includes historic caves once inhabited by early monks.

The Monastery of the Virgin Mary (Gebel El-Teir)

The Monastery of the Virgin Mary (Gebel El-Teir) is an ancient Coptic monastery located east of the Nile in Minya. It is believed that the Holy Family visited the site during their journey through Egypt. The monastery features a rock-hewn church dating back to the 4th century AD, built by Emperor Constantine. It is a significant Christian pilgrimage site, especially during the Virgin Mary’s Fast in August.

Al-Fouli Mosque

Al-Fouli Mosque is a historic mosque in Minya, named after Sheikh Ahmed Al-Fouli, a revered Islamic saint. It is one of the most important mosques in the city, attracting visitors who come to seek blessings at his shrine. The mosque is known for its beautiful Islamic architecture and serves as a religious and cultural center.
