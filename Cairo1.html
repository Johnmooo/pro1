Pyramids of Giza

The Pyramids of Giza are one of the greatest achievements of ancient Egyptian civilization, built over 4,500 years ago as royal tombs for Pharaohs Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure. They are renowned for their precise engineering and massive construction, which continues to puzzle scientists today. The nearby Great Sphinx symbolizes wisdom and strength, reflecting the advanced knowledge of the ancient Egyptians in engineering and astronomy.

Egyptian Museum

The Egyptian Museum in Cairo is one of the world's most important museums, housing the largest collection of Pharaonic artifacts. Opened in 1902 in Tahrir Square, it contains thousands of ancient treasures, including the iconic treasures of King Tutankhamun. The museum showcases the grandeur of ancient Egyptian civilization and is a key destination for history and heritage enthusiasts.

The Saladin Citadel

The Saladin Citadel in Cairo is a historic fortress built in the 12th century to protect the city, strategically located on the Mokattam Hill. It houses the Muhammad Ali Mosque, an Ottoman masterpiece with grand domes and towering minarets. The citadel is a symbol of Egypt’s military and architectural history, offering stunning views of the city.

The Cairo Tower

The Cairo Tower is a modern landmark in the heart of Egypt’s capital, standing 187 meters tall, making it one of the tallest structures in North Africa for a long time. Opened in 1961, its design is inspired by the lotus flower, a symbol of ancient Egyptian civilization. The tower offers a stunning panoramic view of Cairo and features a revolving restaurant, providing visitors with a unique experience of the city from above.

The Hanging Church

The Hanging Church is one of the oldest and most famous Coptic churches in Egypt, located in Old Cairo. Dating back to the 3rd century AD, it is called “Hanging” because it was built above the gate of the Roman Fortress of Babylon. The church is known for its unique architecture and historic icons, making it a significant landmark of Coptic heritage and religious history in Egypt.

The Religious Complex

The Religious Complex in Old Cairo is a unique historical site that houses landmarks representing the three Abrahamic religions: Islam, Christianity, and Judaism. Located in the Fustat district, it includes Amr Ibn Al-As Mosque, the oldest mosque in Africa, the Hanging Church and the Church of Abu Serga, believed to have sheltered the Holy Family, and the Ben Ezra Synagogue. The complex reflects Egypt’s rich history of religious tolerance and cultural diversity over the centuries.
