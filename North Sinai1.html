Tell El-Farama

The archaeological site of Pelusium (Tell El-Farama) in North Sinai is a significant historical location dating back to Pharaonic, Roman, and Islamic periods. Once a major port and trade center, it features ruins of temples, churches, fortresses, and Roman baths, reflecting its strategic importance and rich history.

The Arish Museum

The Arish Museum of Antiquities, located in North Sinai, showcases the region’s history from the Pharaonic to the Islamic periods. It houses a diverse collection of artifacts, including statues, inscriptions, and daily life tools, highlighting Sinai’s role in trade and military campaigns.

Al-Arish Beachl

Al-Arish Beach, located on the Mediterranean coast in North Sinai, is known for its soft white sands and clear blue waters. It is one of Egypt’s most beautiful beaches, offering a peaceful atmosphere for relaxation, surrounded by palm trees that add to its unique charm.

Zaranik Protectorate and Lake Bardawil

Zaranik Protectorate and Lake Bardawil, located in North Sinai, are among Egypt’s most important natural reserves. The protectorate serves as a vital stopover for thousands of migratory birds traveling between Europe and Africa. Lake Bardawil is one of Egypt’s largest saltwater lakes, known for its clear waters and rich fish resources, making it a significant ecological and tourist destination.

Al-Quseima Oasis

Al-Quseima Oasis and Ain Qadeirat, located in North Sinai, are known for their natural beauty and historical significance. Al-Quseima Oasis was an important stop on ancient trade and pilgrimage routes between Egypt and the Levant, featuring remnants of historic fortresses. Ain Qadeirat is one of Sinai’s oldest water sources, surrounded by archaeological remains from the Pharaonic era, believed to be the site of an ancient Egyptian fortress.
