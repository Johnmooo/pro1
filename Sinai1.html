Mount Moses

Mount Moses (Jebel Musa) is one of the most significant religious and historical landmarks in Sinai, believed to be the mountain where Prophet Moses received the Ten Commandments. Standing at approximately 2,285 meters, it is a popular destination for adventure seekers and climbers, with a trail leading to the summit via hundreds of steps or a winding path. The mountain offers a breathtaking view, especially at sunrise, when the sky’s colors blend with the rugged landscape, creating a unique spiritual and natural experience.

Saint Catherine's Monastery

Saint Catherine's Monastery is one of the oldest monasteries in the world, located at the foot of Mount Moses in Sinai. It was founded in the 6th century AD by Emperor Justinian I and is a significant religious and historical site. The monastery houses an ancient church, a library with rare manuscripts, and the sacred Burning Bush, believed to be where Prophet Moses saw the divine fire. With its unique architecture, it attracts pilgrims and tourists from around the world.

Ras Mohammed National

Ras Mohammed National Park is one of Egypt’s most important and beautiful nature reserves, located at the meeting point of the Gulf of Aqaba and the Gulf of Suez in South Sinai. It is known for its unique biodiversity, featuring vibrant coral reefs, rare fish, sea turtles, and mangrove forests, along with underwater caves. The park is a top destination for diving and snorkeling enthusiasts, offering an exceptional experience to explore the rich marine life of the Red Sea’s crystal-clear waters.

Nabq Protected Area

Nabq Protected Area is a unique nature reserve in South Sinai, located between Sharm El Sheikh and Dahab. It is known for its diverse ecosystem, featuring rare mangrove forests, sand dunes, and coral reefs teeming with marine life. The reserve is also home to various wildlife species, including gazelles and foxes. Nabq offers a fantastic experience for nature and adventure lovers, with opportunities for diving, desert trekking, and enjoying breathtaking landscapes.

Ayun Musa

The Springs of Moses (Ayun Musa) are a group of natural freshwater springs located east of the Suez Canal in Sinai. They hold great historical and religious significance, as it is believed that Prophet Moses struck the ground with his staff, causing water to flow for the Israelites during their journey through the desert. The site consists of several springs, some of which still provide fresh water today. Ayun Musa is a popular tourist destination, offering both spiritual significance and beautiful natural scenery.

Wadi Feiran

Wadi Feiran is the largest and most important oasis in South Sinai, known for its lush palm groves and fruit trees. Historically, it served as a key stop for trade caravans and is believed to be one of the stations of the Israelites during their Exodus from Egypt. The oasis also holds religious and historical significance, featuring ancient ruins of churches and monasteries dating back to the Byzantine era. It offers a unique blend of natural beauty and rich heritage.
