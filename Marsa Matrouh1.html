Rommel Beach

Rommel Beach is one of the most beautiful beaches in Marsa Matrouh, known for its clear, calm waters and soft sands. It is located near the Rommel Museum, a historical cave used by the German commander Erwin Rommel during World War II. The beach is a perfect destination for relaxation and enjoying stunning natural scenery.

Cleopatra’s Bath

Cleopatra’s Bath is one of the most famous tourist attractions in Marsa Matrouh. It is a natural rock formation in the sea, where water flows through openings in the rocks, creating a natural bath-like effect. It is said that Queen Cleopatra used to bathe here, which is why it was named after her. The site is known for its crystal-clear turquoise waters and stunning views, making it a perfect destination for nature and history lovers.

Agiba Beach

Agiba Beach is one of the most stunning and unique beaches in Marsa Matrouh, Egypt. The name "Agiba" means "miracle" in Arabic, reflecting its breathtaking beauty. The beach is known for its crystal-clear turquoise waters, soft golden sands, and surrounding limestone cliffs, which create a picturesque and secluded atmosphere. Due to its small size and high waves, it is more suitable for sightseeing and photography rather than swimming. Agiba Beach is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and those seeking a peaceful escape.

Commonwealth War Graves Commission

The Commonwealth War Cemetery in El Alamein is a memorial cemetery for Allied soldiers who lost their lives during the Battle of El Alamein in World War II. Located near Marsa Matrouh, the cemetery contains the remains of thousands of soldiers from various nationalities, including British, Australian, and Indian troops. It features distinctive architectural design and commemorative inscriptions that honor these soldiers. The site is a significant destination for history enthusiasts and those interested in World War history.
