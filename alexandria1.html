Library of alexandria

The Bibliotheca Alexandrina is one of the world's most significant cultural centers. It was inaugurated in 2002 to revive the ancient Library of Alexandria, which was one of the greatest libraries of antiquity. Located in Alexandria, Egypt, the library houses millions of books, as well as museums, research centers, art galleries, and a planetarium. It serves as a symbol of knowledge and cultural exchange, attracting scholars and visitors from around the globe

Citadel of Qaitbay

The Citadel of Qaitbay is a historic fortress located in Alexandria, Egypt. It was constructed between 1477 and 1479 AD by Sultan Al-Ashraf Sayf al-Din Qaitbay on the site of the ancient Lighthouse of Alexandria, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. The citadel served as a defensive stronghold to protect the city from naval attacks. Surrounded by the Mediterranean Sea on three sides, it features impressive architectural elements, including thick walls, defensive towers, and a mosque within its premises. Today, it stands as a significant cultural and historical landmark, attracting visitors from around the world.

Ras el-Tin Palace

Ras El Tin Palace is one of the oldest and most significant palaces in Egypt, located in Alexandria along the Mediterranean coast. Its construction began in 1834 under the orders of Mohamed Ali Pasha and was completed in 1847. The palace was initially designed in a European architectural style, reflecting the influence of foreign communities in Alexandria at the time. It served as a summer residence for the rulers of Egypt and played a key role in the history of the Mohamed Ali dynasty. The palace witnessed pivotal moments, including the abdication of King Farouk in 1952, marking the end of the monarchy in Egypt. Today, it stands as a historical and cultural landmark.

Saint Mark's Cathedral

St. Mark's Cathedral in Alexandria is one of the oldest churches in Africa and serves as the historic seat of the Coptic Orthodox Church. It is believed to be built on the site where St. Mark, the founder of the Coptic Church, was buried in the 1st century AD. Mosque of Abu al-Abbas al-Mursi

A Brief Overview of Abu al-Abbas al-Mursi Mosque in Alexandria Abu al-Abbas al-Mursi Mosque is one of the most famous and beautiful mosques in Alexandria, Egypt. It was built in honor of Abu al-Abbas al-Mursi, a 13th-century Andalusian Sufi saint who was buried in the city. The mosque is renowned for its stunning Islamic architecture, featuring a grand dome, a towering minaret, intricate geometric patterns, and elegant Arabic calligraphy. The mosque's spacious courtyard and ornate interior make it a significant religious and cultural landmark, attracting both worshippers and visitors.

Alexandria jewelry Museum

The Royal Jewelry Museum in Alexandria, Egypt, is housed in the former palace of Princess Fatma Al-Zahraa, a member of the royal family. Built in 1919, the palace showcases European architectural styles and features exquisite decorations, including stained glass windows and gold-plated ceilings. The museum was established in 1986 and contains over 11,500 artifacts, including rare jewelry, gold items, and precious stones that belonged to the royal family of Egypt. It offers visitors a glimpse into the opulence and history of Egypt's monarchy.
