Dababiya Protectorate

Dababiya Protectorate, located in Luxor, is one of Egypt’s most important geological sites. It is known for its unique rock formations dating back to the Paleocene-Eocene era. The site serves as a global reference for studying ancient climate changes, making it an internationally significant stratigraphic benchmark.

Sand dunes

Sand dunes are wind-formed accumulations of sand found in deserts and coastal regions. They come in various shapes, such as crescent, linear, and star dunes, and move over time due to wind activity. They play a crucial ecological role in protecting soil and providing habitats for various organisms.

Hibis Temple

Hibis Temple is an ancient temple located in Kharga Oasis, New Valley Governorate, dating back to the Saite and Persian periods (26th Dynasty). It is one of the most significant temples in Egypt’s Western Desert, known for its well-preserved inscriptions and decorations reflecting the art and religious beliefs of that era. The temple features a hypostyle hall, a sanctuary, and a grand entrance gate, making it a vital historical landmark.

Kharga Oasis

Kharga Oasis is one of the oases in Egypt’s Western Desert and the capital of the New Valley Governorate. It is known for its stunning landscapes, including sand dunes, natural springs, and lush palm groves. The oasis is rich in historical sites such as Hibis Temple, Al-Bagawat Necropolis, and the Fortress of El-Deir,reflecting its long history from Pharaonic to Islamic times. It is also renowned for its tranquil environment and natural resources.

Sulfur Springs

Sulfur Springs are natural water sources rich in sulfur and minerals, found in areas like Kharga Oasis, Siwa, and Fayoum. They are known for their therapeutic benefits, helping treat rheumatism, skin diseases, and improving blood circulation. With varying temperatures, these springs are popular destinations for medical and wellness tourism in Egypt.

The White Desert

The White Desert is located in New Valley Governorate, Egypt, and is one of the most unique and stunning desert landscapes in the world. It is famous for its surreal white rock formations, shaped by wind and erosion over thousands of years, giving it a moon-like appearance. The area is a popular destination for adventure seekers and camping enthusiasts, especially at sunset when the rocks take on breathtaking hues.
