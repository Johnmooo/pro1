The Port Said National Museum

The Port Said National Museum is a prominent cultural landmark in Port Said, Egypt. Situated at the intersection of the Suez Canal and the Mediterranean Sea, it is the first museum of its kind in Egypt's history. The museum houses approximately 9,000 artifacts representing various eras, from the Pharaonic period through the Greek, Roman, Coptic, and Islamic periods, up to the modern era. It reflects the city's history and development over time.

The Old Port Said Lighthouse

The Old Port Said Lighthouse, built in 1869 during the reign of Khedive Ismail, is a historic maritime landmark located at the entrance of the Suez Canal in Port Said, Egypt. It is recognized as the first lighthouse in the world constructed using reinforced concrete. Standing at a height of 56 meters, the lighthouse played a crucial role in guiding ships entering the canal. Its unique architectural design and historical significance make it a symbol of Egypt's maritime heritage.

Ferial Garden

Ferial Garden, located in Port Said, Egypt, is a historic park established in 1869 during the reign of Khedive Ismail. It is named after Princess Ferial, the eldest daughter of King Farouk. The garden is famous for its lush greenery, rare plants, and beautiful flowers. It also features Andalusian-style architecture, a wooden bridge, and decorative columns. Ferial Garden holds historical significance as it hosted the grand opening ceremony of the Suez Canal, attended by kings and dignitaries from around the world.

The Port Said Corniche

The Port Said Corniche is a picturesque waterfront promenade located along the Mediterranean Sea in Port Said, Egypt. It offers stunning views of the sea and the entrance to the Suez Canal. The Corniche is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike, featuring walking paths, seating areas, and vibrant cafes. It is an ideal spot for relaxation, enjoying the sea breeze, and capturing beautiful photographs of the surrounding scenery.
